














 x                                            Stimulants (also often referred to as psychostimulants or colloquially as uppers)
 x                                            is an overarching term that covers many drugs including those that increase activity
 x                                            of the central nervous system and the body, drugs that are pleasurable and invigorating,
 x                                            or drugs that have sympathomimetic effects.

 x                                            Stimulants are widely used throughout the world as prescription medicines as well as without a prescription
 x                                            (either legally or illicitly) as performance-enhancing or recreational drugs. Among narcotics,
 x                                            stimulants produce a noticeable crash or comedown at the end of their effects.

 x                                            The most frequently prescribed stimulants as of 2013 were lisdexamfetamine (Vyvanse),
 x                                            methylphenidate (Ritalin), and amphetamine (Adderall).

 x                                            It was estimated in 2015 that the percentage of the world population that used cocaine during a year (0.4%).

 x                                            (with "amphetamines" including amphetamine and methamphetamine) the value was 0.7%, and for MDMA 0.4%.
